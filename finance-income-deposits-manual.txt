This manual covers the two resources in Finance — Income & Deposits:
  1. Member Deposits   (Sales Invoices / Credit Memos)
  2. Receipts          (Cash Receipts)

These two screens represent the INCOME side of the double-entry ledger.
Member Deposits generate the receivable (what a member owes). Receipts
record the actual cash received and close out the receivable.

PREREQUISITE: Finance — Setup must be fully configured before using
these screens. See finance-setup-manual.txt.

DOCUMENT FLOW:
  Create Invoice → Post Invoice → Customer Ledger Entry (open) created
  Create Receipt → Post Receipt → Customer Ledger Entry (closed)
                                → GL Entry (bank debit, receivable credit)

================================================================================
  1. MEMBER DEPOSITS (Sales Invoices)
  Nav path: Finance — Income & Deposits → Member Deposits
================================================================================

WHAT IT IS:
  A sales document (invoice or credit memo) issued to a customer (member).
  It represents a formal demand for payment. Before posting it exists as
  a draft. Once posted it becomes permanent and creates entries in the
  Customer Ledger and GL.

  ALSO NOTE: Share subscription invoices and SBF claim-related invoices
  are created here. The share_subscription_id column links an invoice back
  to a Chakama share subscription when generated by the system.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  1.1 CREATING A SALES INVOICE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Navigate to: Finance — Income & Deposits → Member Deposits → New

  SECTION: Document Header

  Document No      : Auto-generated when saved (from Sales Setup →
                     Invoice Number Series). Shown as blank on the
                     create screen — it is assigned on first save.

  Document Type    : Invoice     → standard bill to a member
                     Credit Memo → reversal / refund document
                     Select the correct type before saving — it cannot
                     be changed after the record is created.

  Customer         : Search and select the customer (member).
                     Typing searches by name or customer number.
                     Selecting a customer auto-fills the Customer
                     Posting Group below.

  Customer Posting Group: Auto-filled from the selected customer's
                     record. Read-only. Determines the Receivables GL
                     account for this invoice.

  Posting Date     : The date the invoice will be recorded in the ledger
                     when posted. Defaults to today. Can be backdated.

  Due Date         : Payment due date. Optional. Used for overdue tracking
                     and aging reports. Does not affect posting.

  Status           : Open   → draft, not yet posted (editable)
                     Posted → permanent, cannot be changed

  SECTION: Invoice Lines (one row per service being billed)

  Each line requires:
    Service        : Select from Sellable services. Selecting a service
                     auto-fills Description and Unit Price from the
                     service record.
    Description    : Editable after auto-fill. Appears on the member's
                     invoice/statement.
    Quantity       : Number of units (e.g. 1, 3, 12). Must be > 0.
                     For share subscriptions: quantity = number of shares.
    Unit Price     : Price per unit in KES. Auto-filled from service;
                     editable.
    Line Amount    : Calculated automatically = Quantity × Unit Price.
                     Read-only. Updates live as you change Qty or Price.

  Multiple lines can be added (click + Add Line).
  Lines can be deleted with the trash icon while status = Open.

  SAVE: Click Save / Create. Document No is assigned.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  1.2 EDITING A DRAFT INVOICE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  An invoice can be edited (header and lines) ONLY while Status = Open.
  To edit: click the record → click [Edit].

  Once posted, the edit button is hidden and the record is locked.
  To correct a posted invoice, create a Credit Memo against it.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  1.3 POSTING A SALES INVOICE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Posting makes the invoice permanent and creates the accounting entries.

  HOW TO POST:
    From the list view: click the green [✓ Post] action button on the row.
    From the detail view: the same action appears as a header button.

  WHAT HAPPENS ON POSTING:
    1. Status changes to Posted.
    2. A new Document No is assigned from the Posted Invoice Number Series
       (different from the draft number).
    3. A Customer Ledger Entry is created:
          document_type  = Invoice
          document_no    = posted invoice no
          amount         = total invoice amount
          remaining_amount = same as amount (unpaid)
          is_open        = true
    4. GL entries are created for each line:
          DR  Receivables Account  (from Customer Posting Group)
          CR  Revenue Account      (from Service Posting Group of each line)
    5. The invoice cannot be edited or deleted after this point.

  PRECONDITIONS FOR POSTING:
    - Customer must have a Customer Posting Group assigned.
    - Each service line must have a valid Service Posting Group with a
      Revenue Account No configured.
    - Posting Date must be set.
    - At least one line must exist.

  If posting fails, check the above. The most common cause is a missing
  Revenue Account No on a Service Posting Group.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  1.4 CREDIT MEMOS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  A Credit Memo reverses or reduces a previously posted invoice.

  CREATING A CREDIT MEMO:
    New → Document Type = Credit Memo
    Fill in the same customer and lines as the original invoice (or a
    partial subset for a partial reversal).
    Post it.

  WHAT HAPPENS ON POSTING A CREDIT MEMO:
    - A Customer Ledger Entry is created with a NEGATIVE amount.
    - The Apply Entries process (Finance — Ledgers → Customer Ledger)
      can be used to close the original invoice against the credit memo.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  1.5 TABLE COLUMNS & FILTERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Table columns on the Member Deposits list:
    Document No      : Draft or posted number (searchable)
    Type             : Invoice / Credit Memo (badge)
    Customer         : Member name (searchable, sortable)
    Posting Date     : Date (sortable; default sort = newest first)
    Due Date         : Payment deadline
    Status           : Open (amber) / Posted (green)

  FILTERING:
    Use the Status filter to view only Open invoices (outstanding) or
    only Posted invoices.
    The search box matches Document No and Customer name simultaneously.


================================================================================
  2. RECEIPTS (Cash Receipts)
  Nav path: Finance — Income & Deposits → Receipts
================================================================================

WHAT IT IS:
  A Cash Receipt records actual money received from a member (by M-PESA,
  bank transfer, or cash). Once posted, it applies to the member's open
  Customer Ledger entries, reducing the outstanding balance.

  For Chakama share subscriptions: the share_subscription_id column on
  a receipt links it back to a specific share subscription, triggering
  automatic payment tracking and status updates.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  2.1 CREATING A CASH RECEIPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Navigate to: Finance — Income & Deposits → Receipts → New

  Receipt No       : Auto-generated on save from Sales Setup →
                     Receipt Number Series (e.g. REC-000001).

  Customer         : Search and select the paying member.

  Payment Method   : Select the method (M-PESA, Bank Transfer, Cash, etc.)
                     The linked Default Bank Account auto-fills below.

  Bank Account     : Auto-filled from the Payment Method's default.
                     Can be overridden if the payment landed in a
                     different account.

  Posting Date     : Date of receipt. Defaults to today.

  Amount           : Total amount received in KES. Must match what was
                     actually received.

  Description      : Reference note (M-PESA transaction ID, cheque no,
                     bank reference, etc.) — important for reconciliation.

  M-PESA Receipt No: If payment method = M-PESA, capture the MPESA
                     confirmation code here.

  M-PESA Phone     : Sending phone number (for M-PESA traceability).

  Status           : Open → draft. Posted → permanent.

  SAVE: Click Save. Receipt No is assigned.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  2.2 POSTING A CASH RECEIPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  HOW TO POST:
    From the list: click the green [✓ Post] button on the row.
    From detail view: same button in the header.

  WHAT HAPPENS ON POSTING:
    1. Status changes to Posted.
    2. GL entries are created:
          DR  Bank Account GL         (from Bank Account → Bank Posting Group)
          CR  Receivables Account     (from Customer's Customer Posting Group)
    3. Open Customer Ledger Entries for this customer are reduced:
          The receipt amount is matched against the oldest open invoice(s)
          first. When an invoice's remaining_amount reaches 0, is_open = false.
    4. If this receipt is linked to a share subscription (share_subscription_id):
          ShareBillingService::recordPayment() runs:
            → sub.amount_paid += receipt.amount
            → if fully paid: subscription status → Active automatically
    5. The receipt cannot be edited after posting.

  PRECONDITIONS:
    - Customer must be set.
    - Payment Method and Bank Account must be set.
    - Amount must be > 0.
    - Bank Account must have a Bank Posting Group configured.

  COMMON ERROR:
    "Bank Posting Group not configured" — go to Finance — Setup →
    Bank Accounts and ensure a Bank Posting Group is assigned.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  2.3 TABLE COLUMNS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Receipt No       : Auto-number (searchable)
  Customer         : Member name (searchable, sortable)
  Payment Method   : Method used
  Bank Account     : Destination account
  Posting Date     : Date (default sort = newest first)
  Amount           : KES received
  Status           : Open (amber) / Posted (green)

  The list shows all receipts regardless of whether they are linked to
  share subscriptions or to SBF claims.


================================================================================
  DOCUMENT LIFECYCLE SUMMARY
================================================================================

  STEP  SCREEN               ACTION           RESULT
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1     Member Deposits      Create Invoice   Draft invoice (Open)
  2     Member Deposits      Post Invoice     Customer Ledger entry created
                                              GL entries: DR Receivable,
                                                          CR Revenue
  3     Receipts             Create Receipt   Draft receipt (Open)
  4     Receipts             Post Receipt     GL entries: DR Bank,
                                                          CR Receivable
                                              Customer Ledger entry closed
                                              (if fully paid)

  For share subscriptions:
  Step 4 also → sub.amount_paid += amount
              → if fully paid → sub.status = Active


================================================================================
  GL IMPACT REFERENCE
================================================================================

  EVENT                     DEBIT                    CREDIT
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Post Sales Invoice        Receivables Account      Revenue Account
                            (Customer Posting Grp)   (Service Posting Grp)

  Post Cash Receipt         Bank GL Account          Receivables Account
                            (Bank Posting Grp)       (Customer Posting Grp)

  Post Credit Memo          Revenue Account          Receivables Account
  (Reversal)                (Service Posting Grp)    (Customer Posting Grp)
